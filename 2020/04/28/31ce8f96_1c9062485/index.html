I really admire Steven
Created 2020-04-28| Updated 2020-04-28|Text

I really admire Steven. I know the pressure he is under every day, everybody talking about him all day long. 

He’s a great example, and those of us who have been in a similar situation know how difficult it is to handle. 

I’d love to be captain of another team one day and Steve has shown me how to be a great leader.”

我很爱慕史蒂文。我知道他每天在肩上背负的压力，每个人整天都在谈论他。

他是一个很好的典范，我们都有相似的处境知道这是多难应付的。

史蒂文展示出如何当一个好领袖，我乐意他日在别的队伍担任队长。

自传好像也差不多的发言，暂不翻完整的

相识一年的表白x，托妞这么喜欢拉德的其中一个原因永远离不开当队长的压力和曾经相似的处景，拉德都叫Captain Fantastic还需要说明什么吗

而你托不愧是预言boy，长大后在乌栖当队长，日本的记录片当中则说过因为曾经和很好的队长，比如拉德和老蒋相处过，让我学会怎样当队长和鼓励队友


20080708

“You must always push yourself and never become complacent”

The Liverpool and Spanish striker looked back over his time at Liverpool and his first steps in football with Atletico Madrid in an interview with ‘Champions Magazine’.

The striker holds fond memories of his first professional coach. “I started playing when I was five and I have had a lot of trainers since then. My first professional coach was Manolo Rangel at Atletico Madrid. I was ten years old and he was the first person who told me that I could play at the highest level. He also told me that I should never stop enjoying playing football. He used to select one player a day to choose what they wanted to do in training. Manolo would always ask: “What do you want us to do today”. With the passing of time, you forget the details, but what he told us about always enjoying the game is still with me today. I also learnt that you must give 100% in every training session, but above all, sport is to be enjoyed.”

Fernando spoke of the difficulty faced by referees week-in, week-out. “I have learnt that you do not play against the referee. We all try to do our job out on the pitch, both footballers and referees. I have spoken with referees and have also enjoyed conversations with them about football for over 30 minutes. We should not moan, complain or protest too much, because at the end of the day, they are just trying to do their best. For example, I remember on one occasion I was insulted during a game, the referee then came up to me and to say, ‘that is what I have to live with every weekend.’ I had never really given it much thought, but now I try to help them as much as possible.”

The Liverpool striker spoke fondly of his ex-team and the warm welcome he hopes to receive the next time he returns to the Vicente Calderon Stadium. “I am an Atletico Madrid supporter and I would love to return one day. Liverpool might even meet them in the Champions League! I would like to think that I would receive a warm welcome from the Atletico supporters. Whenever I bump into an Atletico fan in the street, they come up to me and tell me that they still consider me ‘one of them’; it is a great feeling. It would be tough if I returned to the Calderon and did not receive a warm welcome. It would not be easy to cope with, but I hope that it will never happen.”

Torres also had kind words for the Liverpool fans. “I could not imagine a better set of supporters. They are always 100% behind the team, throughout the good and bad times. Regardless of what happens, they never stop supporting either myself or the team. As soon as you pull-on the red shirt, they are behind you. They do not get on your back if you make a mistake because you are wearing the Liverpool shirt, and they support the team, and not the name on the shirt. They enjoy watching their team play and they respect ex-players who return to Anfield playing for other teams. When things go well, they love their players, and when things don’t work out, they show their support. The players always feel the fans’ support and we really appreciate it. That is the major difference between England and Spain.”

The Reds striker netted 33 goals in all-competitions to finish with the best ever tally in a debut campaign for a foreign player and he believes that the Liverpool manager played an important role.

“There are days when you think: ‘My God, Benitez doesn’t let you breathe’. At times like that, you don't realise that all he wants is for you to improve. When you are in a team where things are going mostly well, players tend to relax. But you must always push yourself, and never become complacent; you try to do your best every single day.”

“At a club like Liverpool, you can't sit back after scoring 20-odd goals and say: ‘Well, that's it.’ After you score 20, Benitez is at you during the next training session and stays on top of you all day, all week. He tells you to go off on your own and do new exercises. I want to progress in life, not relax, not be complacent, and you need someone close to you telling you to keep at it. It is impossible to do it otherwise. We don’t always fancy someone that close but, in the long term, I am sure that everybody will thank him for it.”

Fernando Torres also had words of praise for Liverpool skipper Steven Gerrard, whose conduct both on and off the pitch has served as a perfect example for the striker. “I really admire Steven. I know the pressure he is under every day, everybody talking about him all day long. In the changing-room, in the pubs, in the stadium. I’ve heard people say he should be stronger, have a stronger personality. I know by experience how difficult it is to deal with that because Liverpool is a huge club. And he’s at another level, of course.”

“People look at you from a distance and challenge you with their eyes. All that happens to Steve every day and he copes with it, everything that is expected of him all the time. He’s always under tremendous pressure to perform and everyone looks to him to show leadership, to lift the team.”

“He’s a great example, and those of us who have been in a similar situation know how difficult it is to handle. It’s incredible the way he carries himself regardless of what is going on around him. I’d love to be captain of another team one day and Steve has shown me how to be a great leader.”
