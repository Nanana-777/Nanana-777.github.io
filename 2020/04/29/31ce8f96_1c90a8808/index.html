Torres: “Liverpool had everything I needed for me to continue developing as a player”

Welcome to the first part of Marca’s exclusive interview with Fernando Torres. The Spanish daily sports newspaper dedicated three pages of Saturday’s edition, including the front page, to the Liverpool and Spain number ‘9’. The striker, who continues to capture the headlines and front pages in his native Spain, talks openly in the interview about Liverpool, Spain and Atletico Madrid.

- What hurt you more? The injury to your leg, or missing the games versus Atletico?

Right now not being able to face Atletico, and even more so after seeing the great atmosphere there was at the Vicente Calderon and at Anfield. It was a fixture that really appealed to me. I was looking forward to returning to Madrid, and later I though that I would be back in time for the return match. Now though I feel much better.

it a penalty (Liverpool’s equaliser versus Atletico)?

Watching the game live, it all happened very quickly. It was the linesman who raised his flag. There must have been some contact, because Steve Gerrard isn’t one to take a dive. I don’t know if there was enough contact for the ref to point to the spot, but at the end of the day it was a good decision for us.

-Fate did not want you to face your old club…

Yes, that’s how it turned out. First there was the possible stadium ban to the Vicente Calderon and then the injury. These things happen for a reason. In the end, both teams drew, and we are both close to qualifying for the next round… so both sides are happy.

-Would you have celebrated had you have scored against Atletico Madrid?

No, I wouldn’t have celebrated, though I don’t think that this was one of the best kept secrets. I grew up there and I owe a lot to the club. I have a lot of respect for Atletico fans and it would have been stupid to have celebrated a goal there. There would have been no need and I wouldn’t have felt like celebrating. However, I still would have given my all to score, to win, and to play as well as possible.

- Your departure from Atletico Madrid coincided with your career literally taking-off: now you are in the running for the Golden Ball, 2008 European Champions… Was there a reason for why Atletico Madrid and Fernando Torres did not manage to make the leap together?

Yes. I needed a change. I was shouldering too much responsibility, both on and off the pitch, and I needed to get away from it; to concentrate on playing football and to learn from great players (in every aspect of the game). I got given the perfect chance: to play alongside fellow Spaniards, a Spanish coach who gave me his full backing, a great captain (one of the best in the world). Liverpool had everything I needed for me to continue developing as a player. There are some clubs which are the biggest in Europe, and it’s these clubs that take part in the most important games. That’s where the main difference lies.

- Would you like to retire at Atletico Madrid?

I don’t know. I still want to experience many things, but Atletico is an exceptional club. I would love to return one day, though I don’t know if I would retire from football there. But I would like to go back and finish some of things I could not do whilst there. There was nothing more I could do for the club when I left, but yes, I would like to go back.

- Have you thought about returning to play in Spain?

Not yet. I am playing in a competitive league. I am enjoying my football, and I am also left to work and train without any real hastle.
