Playing a mind game
Created 2020-04-25| Updated 2020-04-25|Text

Phil Thompson says the telepathy between Steven Gerrard and Fernando Torres will help sink Chelsea.

By Phil Thompson

Last Updated: 08/04/09 11:04am

Liverpool should be confident about their chances of beating Chelsea over two legs - but not over-confident.

The way things have panned out for them over the last six weeks I would think that they are in a very confident position. Chelsea have had some good results, particularly at Newcastle at the weekend, but Liverpool are on such a roll now that they are becoming unstoppable.

They are playing some really good, confident football. That quality of the flowing football they played against Real Madrid and Manchester United and Aston Villa was fantastic.

I thought that was even more evident in what was an outstanding performance against Fulham, where they could have scored four or five. As it was, Yossi Benayoun's late winner has given them an extra lift ahead of the Champions League game.

The players celebrated like madmen; they knew the importance of the goal and the togetherness that it brought. It was total mayhem among the players and the fans and I expect to see some of that exuberance in evidence against Chelsea.

Key

The key for Liverpool at the moment is the movement of Fernando Torres and Steven Gerrard. It makes them look telepathic; they don't even need to look to know what each other is doing.

They remind me very much of the Kenny Dalglish-Ian Rush partnership, where Rushie would just take off and Kenny didn't even look to find him.

He wouldn't play it to the man but just into the space behind the defenders knowing that Rush would already be on his way. Torres is like that as soon as Gerrard gets on the ball.

Dare I say it, I wouldn't be surprised if Chelsea go man-for-man on Gerrard and put John Obi Mikel on him in some outrageous capacity where he just follows him around all day.

But you can't keep Gerrard quiet for the whole of the game no matter what you do because he is so quick he can get away from any marker in the world.

Form

Liverpool look in good form from the back to the front at the moment and I don't think Chelsea have been 100% solid at the back.

They've had a lot of injuries: Ricardo Carvalho has struggled for fitness this season and Alex has come in so they've had a few changes in defence. Jose Bosingwa's just been off and they don't look as solid as they have been in recent seasons.

I wouldn't be surprised if Chelsea leave Nicolas Anelka out and go with a 4-5-1 formation. They can use Didier Drogba as the target man and I would expect them to bring Michael Ballack back in midfield. If Guus Hiddink goes 4-4-2 that will play into Liverpool's hands.

They are in good form but lack fluency, which in a way is typical Chelsea; they are very, very hard to beat and like Arsenal they are building momentum.

Cagey

I think it will be a cagey first leg; yes, it is a European tie but this is two English teams who have great respect for each other and know what each is capable of.

With a non-English team you can go for the jugular and frighten the life out of them, whereas English clubs are more willing to soak up the pressure and accept it.

But as we know the pressure can build on anyone at Anfield to the point where it all becomes unbearable for the opposition.

Although I think Liverpool will win 2-0 at home, the tie certainly won't be over after the first leg. I expect the return fixture to be a draw - probably 1-1.

THOMMO'S CALL: 2-0 to Liverpool.
