Torres: “People at Liverpool are looking out for Spain”

Fernando Torres was the special guest on the Spanish radio programme ‘El Larguero’ (the Crossbar). During the interview the striker spoke about the national team, the upcoming European Championships and life at Liverpool.

- We have heard that the first team squad spend a lot of time at Melwood, but you also spend a lot more time with the reserve team… something almost unheard of back in Spain. Do the youth players clean boots?

We spend all day with the youth players. The first team and the reserves pass a lot of time together, almost as if we were all part of the same squad. The youth players don’t clean your boots for an example. The tradition here is that every player has to clean his own boots after training before leaving them in their locker. One of the differences though can be seen when it comes to the car park. The parking places are all numbered and the reserve team players cannot park in the first 37 numbers, as they are reserved for the first team.

- How was the busy playing schedule over Christmas?

There were a lot of games during the Christmas period, but things went a lot better than I expected. I thought that it would be harder. I think that the most important thing is the people you have around you…. because if you are happy, it does not matter where you are.

- Have you felt under a lot of pressure?

The transfer fee means a greater responsibility, and a lot of time this can lead to people asking for results straight away. Luckily they have arrived and everything has been easier. If events had been different, then maybe I would have felt under more pressure.

- How is Rafa?

Rafa is proud of the fans’ support and the recognition he receives from those around him, which for a professional, is the biggest reward. It is unbelievable how well people treat him here. I have never seen anything like it before. A lot has been written about Rafa, but he is fine and fully focused on this season. I don’t think there is a problem there.

- How far do you feel Spain can go in Euro 2008?

“We are looking forward to the event, as we have a good squad with good players, but we have to see what unfolds when the tournament kicks-off. We don’t need to be seen as favourites, instead we have to move forward step by step. The team are all focused on reaching as far as possible as we all want to lift the trophy. We know that we are capable of winning it, but we should not go out there too confident because that could lead you to arriving for the tournament over complacent. If we do not work as hard as the other sides, we will come away empty-handed. I remember that following the first few games at the last World Cup, we all thought that we could go on to win the tournament… so you should always keep your feet on the ground and respect your rivals.”

- How is the Spanish side seen abroad?

“A lot of colleagues have asked me if we believe that we are in with a chance of winning this summer. They are actually quite surprised that Spain never seem to win any silverware. Now that England are not going to Euro 2008, people at Liverpool are looking out for Spain. To miss out on the championships was a big blow for everybody here in England and you can see just how hard it is for them to not be part of the event. Players like Gerrard, who is also the captain of the national team and carries a lot of responsibility, need to participate in these kinds of tournaments if they want to reach the very top of the game; otherwise they must feel like they are missing out on something. What has happened here though, can happen to anyone; you just have to learn from these things.”

- What about Andres Iniesta? (The two players have worked their way up the Spanish youth teams together)
