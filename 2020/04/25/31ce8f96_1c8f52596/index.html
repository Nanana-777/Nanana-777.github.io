TORRES: KOP FLOP WAS SO HARD ON GERRARD
Created 2020-04-25| Updated 2020-04-25|Text

FERNANDO TORRES last night insisted Liverpool skipper Steve Gerrard was more hurt about last ­season’s Kop flop than any Anfield supporter.

The Spanish superstar striker reckons Gerro regards the ­Liverpool fans as family – and is still as desperate as ever to win the Premier League title with his boyhood club.

nd Torres also fired a shot at England boss Fabio Capello, ­telling the England coach he should have made Gerrard his captain instead of John Terry and Rio Ferdinand.


Liverpool finished seventh last season after finishing second the previous year.


Looking back, Torres, 26, said: “This season Liverpool have been ­written and talked about a lot.


“But what people forget is that fundamentally last season hurt the players and the boss.

“The club means a lot to us and the fans mean a lot to us. We didn’t want to give them a season like that.


“Trust me when I tell you nobody will be hurting more than Stevie.


“He is Mr Liverpool and he sees the Liverpool fans as ­family. Last season really hurt him.


“He desperately wants to win the the Premier League with ­ Liverpool and every season that he does not do it hurts a bit more.


“He is a player who plays on ­passion and emotions.


“Liverpool’s poor season can be England’s gain. Stevie will want to end this season on a high and I think Liverpool’s season will give him even more desire than he ­normally has.


“Stevie playing normally is one of the top five players in the world – but a hurt Stevie can be ­unstoppable.”


And Torres is convinced Gerrard can lead from the front after ­stepping in to skipper the team ­following Ferdinand’s injury.

He added: “Stevie is the right choice as captain.

“With all due respect I would have made him captain anyway ­before the Rio Ferdinand injury.

“He is the best captain I have ever played under. As long as we don’t meet earlier, I see it being an ­England versus Spain final.

“They have fantastic players all over the pitch. I have been telling the Spanish lads all about them.

“I see Gerrard and Rooney on the same level as Messi, Xavi, Kaka, Ronaldo and the other top players.

“It would be a dream to play England in the final and an ­England-Spain final would be a showcase of the two best nations in world football.”

As for Liverpool’s departed boss Rafa Benitez, Torres said: “I have nothing but respect for Rafa and he has taught me a lot.

“But I can’t think about that ­situation now. I am not sure who the Liverpool manager will be but I need to stay focused on Spain.”
